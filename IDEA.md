## The Idea

Every day, billions of things are created — code, articles, designs,
products, arguments, plans. And every day, millions of those things
are copies of something that already exists, dressed in different
words, different names, different surfaces.

No one can tell.

Not search engines — they match words, and copies use different words.
Not plagiarism checkers — they compare text, and structural copies
change every sentence. Not AI detectors — they guess whether a machine
wrote it, not whether the idea already existed. Not human beings — we
are wired to see surfaces, not structures.

**Firstprint sees structure.**

It takes any digital artifact, strips away the surface — the names,
the formatting, the language, the styling — and reveals the structural
skeleton: the logic, the flow, the relationships, the architecture.
The thing underneath that makes it what it is.

Then it answers two questions:

**"Am I first?"** — Before you invest time building something, know
instantly if the same structural approach already exists anywhere on
the internet. In any language. In any framework. With any naming.

**"Can I prove it?"** — The moment you create something, Firstprint
gives it a cryptographic birth certificate — signed, timestamped,
chained. If anyone reproduces your structural patterns later, you have
machine-verifiable proof you were first.

One input. One click. Two answers that change everything.

## The Engine

Structural fingerprinting via AST normalization, feature extraction
across four layers (logic, flow, dependencies, architecture), MinHash
and SimHash for similarity-preserving comparison, rarity-weighted
scoring that ignores common patterns and highlights rare structural
overlaps, Ed25519 signed provenance ledger with Merkle chain integrity.

## The Experience

Browser extension. VS Code extension. API. Paste or highlight
anything. One click. Seconds. No registration required.

The public internet is the starting registry. The first user gets
full value on their first query. No cold start. No network needed.
Millions of public repos, billions of functions, already out there,
already parseable.

## The Flywheel

Every query builds the world's first structural registry — a map of
what has been built, how it works, and when it was created. The more
people use it, the more comprehensive the map, the more valuable
every query.

## The Moat

The rarity model requires neutral aggregation of structural patterns
across competing platforms. No single AI company will share that data
with rivals. Only Firstprint can build it.
